About
the project

The Wretched of the Earth (Les Damnés de la Terre) is a landmark work of postcolonial theory written by psychiatrist and revolutionary thinker Frantz Fanon in the final months of his life, during the Algerian War of Independence. Published in 1961 with a preface by Jean-Paul Sartre, the book remains one of the most influential political texts of the twentieth century.

PROJECT DESCRIPTION

This project explores the semantic representation of *The Wretched of the Earth* by Frantz Fanon and its broader postcolonial intellectual context through Semantic Web technologies and Digital Humanities methodologies. The project models people, concepts, places, events, organizations, and textual entities related to the work in order to formalise their historical, political, and cultural relations. The workflow combines theoretical modelling, E/R modelling, ontology design, metadata alignment, TEI encoding, and RDF transformation. The RDF dataset reuses the CIDOC-CRM and FRBRoo ontologies and reconciles entities to external authority files including VIAF, Wikidata, GeoNames, WorldCat, and Getty AAT, while the metadata alignment stage maps the model to established metadata standards such as MARC21 and Dublin Core (DCTerms), in order to support interoperability and Linked Open Data integration. The resulting semantic structure enables the representation of bibliographic, conceptual, and historical knowledge through RDF triples and ontology-based relations, transforming the selected corpus into a machine-readable and interoperable knowledge graph.

Person

Frantz Fanon

Martinican psychiatrist, philosopher and revolutionary. Joined the FLN in Algeria. His work combines psychoanalysis, Marxism and anticolonial thought. Died of leukaemia at 36.

Wikipedia ↗
Person

Jean-Paul Sartre

French existentialist philosopher. Wrote the controversial preface to the 1961 edition, endorsing Fanon's argument on the liberatory potential of anticolonial violence.

Wikipedia ↗
Person

Homi K. Bhabha

Indian-British postcolonial theorist at Harvard University. Wrote the foreword to the 2004 edition, reinterpreting Fanon through poststructuralism. Key concepts: hybridity, mimicry, ambivalence.

Wikipedia ↗
Person

Aimé Césaire

Martinican poet, playwright and politician. Co-founder of the Négritude movement and a major intellectual influence on Fanon, who studied under him.

TEI → HTML / RDF Pipeline

The source text — based on the English Wikipedia article on The Wretched of the Earth — was encoded in TEI/XML, with persons, places, organisations, events, concepts and titles annotated and reconciled to authority files (VIAF, Wikidata, GeoNames, WorldCat, Getty AAT). The TEI document is transformed to HTML through an XSLT stylesheet (and an equivalent Python script), and to RDF triples through a Python script that maps the TEI stand-off markup to the CIDOC-CRM / FRBRoo ontologies.
