Knowledge Organisation — Metadata Alignment

After identifying the entities and their corresponding metadata standards, the metadata alignment phase established semantic interoperability between the different resources included in the project. The final adopted standards include Dublin Core Terms (DCTerms), MARC21, CIDOC-CRM, and Schema.org, selected according to the type of entity and relation represented in the dataset.

Dublin Core Terms and Schema.org were used for general descriptive properties related to authorship, publication, titles, places, and dates. MARC21 was primarily adopted for bibliographic entities such as The Wretched of the Earth and "On Violence," while CIDOC-CRM was used for more complex cultural and semantic relations involving events, participation, influence, and conceptual connections.

The alignment process was structured around four semantic dimensions: WHO, WHAT, WHERE, and WHEN. Additional project-specific relations such as critiques, advocates, theorises, participated in, and originated from were aligned through CIDOC-CRM properties in order to support a richer semantic representation and future RDF transformation.
