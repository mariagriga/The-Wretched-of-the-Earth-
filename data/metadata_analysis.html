Knowledge Organisation — Metadata Analysis

All selected metadata standards contribute to the semantic representation and interoperability of the entities related to The Wretched of the Earth. Since the project combines bibliographic resources, people, places, concepts, organizations, and historical events, different standards and authority systems were adopted according to the nature of each entity.

The book The Wretched of the Earth and the essay "On Violence" were associated with WorldCat records and described through the MARC21 standard, as both entities belong to the bibliographic domain and are primarily represented through library cataloguing systems. Person entities such as Frantz Fanon, Jean-Paul Sartre, and Aimé Césaire were reconciled through VIAF authority files, allowing the integration of standardized personal metadata commonly used in library and archival environments. Homi K. Bhabha and Grove Press were represented through Wikidata, whose schema provides flexible linked open data descriptions for both intellectual figures and organizations.

Geographical entities such as Algeria and Martinique were connected to GeoNames in order to provide standardized spatial identification and geographical interoperability. Historical events including the Algerian Revolution and the Second Congress of Black Writers and Artists were represented through Wikidata schema because of its suitability for modelling complex historical and cultural relations.

Conceptual entities required a different metadata approach. Colonialism and Decolonisation were associated with Getty AAT vocabularies and described through the CDWA standard, which is widely adopted for the description of cultural heritage concepts and thematic categories. Négritude was instead represented through Wikidata schema due to its hybrid intellectual, literary, and political nature.

The selection of metadata standards reflects the heterogeneous nature of the project and aims to ensure semantic consistency across bibliographic, geographical, conceptual, and historical entities while supporting future RDF transformation and linked open data interoperability.
